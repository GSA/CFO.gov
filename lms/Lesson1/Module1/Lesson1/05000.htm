Digital Accountability and Transparency Act (DATA) of 2014
(P.L. 113-101)

Statute/ActFederal Agency
The DATA Act amends the Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act of 2006 (FFATA). It requires the Department of the Treasury and the White House Office of Management and Budget to improve and expand the data available on the publicly accessible website created under FFATA. Under the DATA Act, reporting is required for all awards. When implemented, Federal agencies will standardize and publish a variety of data related to spending: financial management, payments, budget actions, procurement, and assistance.

IN PROCESS: Between 2014 and 2017 Federal agencies will receive guidance, participate in pilots, provide guidance to staff and recipients, and ensure that the data reporting will meet the required standards.

Reference: Digital Accountability and Transparency Act (DATA) of 2014
