Antideficiency Act

The Antideficiency Act, 31 U.S.C. § 1341(a), prohibits Federal agencies from obligating or expending funds in excess of the appropriated amounts.

The Antideficiency Act is one of the primary "enforcement devices." It has been termed "the cornerstone of Congressional efforts to bind the Executive branch of government to the limits on expenditure of appropriated funds."

Its importance is underscored by both civil and criminal penalties for violation.
