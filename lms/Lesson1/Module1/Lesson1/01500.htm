Within the Time Limit

The placing of time limits on the availability of appropriations is one means of congressional control.

The period of availability of appropriated funds is the period of time provided by law in which the Federal agency has to obligate the funds (e.g., generally the funds are obligated when the Federal agency and recipient sign the grant agreement).

Funds must be obligated by the Federal agency within their period of availability.

Appropriations may be classified based on duration.

  • One-year appropriation
  • Multiple-year appropriation
  • No-year appropriation
