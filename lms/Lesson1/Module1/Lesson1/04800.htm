FFATA of 2006, as Amended by the Government Funding Transparency Act of 2008 (continued)

The Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act of 2006 (FFATA) increases the transparency of and accountability for the over $1 trillion that Federal agencies award each year in contracts, loans, grants, and other financial assistance.

The intent of the FFATA is to increase the transparency of government spending through Federal awards. The FFATA legislation requires that information on Federal awards made through transactions over $25,000 be made available to the public via a single, searchable website. In answer to this legislation, www.USASpending.gov was established.
