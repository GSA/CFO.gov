Mission History

The CFO Act of 1990 sought to improve Federal financial management practices by outlining standards of performance and transparency. The Act also assigned OMB greater responsibility for overseeing Federal financial management and it established the Chief Financial Officer's Council (CFOC) to directly advise and coordinate Financial Management activities of the agencies.

Mission

Option 1: The CFO Council is a forum of Chief Financial Officers (CFOs) and Deputy CFOs of the largest Federal agencies, senior officials of the Office of Management and Budget (OMB), and the Department of the Treasury who work collaboratively to improve financial management across the U.S. Government.

Option 2: The CFOC is an organization of the CFOs and Deputy CFOs of the largest Federal agencies, senior officials of the Office of Management and Budget, and the Department of the Treasury who work collaboratively to improve financial management in the U.S. Government. The Council was established under the provisions of the CFO Act of 1990 to advise and coordinate the activities of the member agencies on such matters as consolidation and modernization of financial systems, improved quality of financial information, financial data and information standards, internal controls, legislation affecting financial operations and organizations, and any other financial management matter.

Strategy

A primary responsibility of the CFOC is overseeing the implementation of the President's Financial Management Agenda (PFMA) - as well as supporting other key Financial Management initiatives. Collectively, these CFOC-sponsored initiatives may also be referred to as the "Financial Management Transformation Strategy."
